December 21, 2023 | 1:15pm

Afternoon Update

It's good to be back.

The mountain is skiing surprisingly well given the circumstances and that's in large part due to our incredible team members. The run of the day was Hayburner but Haywire was a close second. They were firm and smooth with great coverage. It was very edgeable snow - I truly got some of my best turns of the whole year. Tote Road was variable with snow inconsistencies but still enjoyable. Boardwalk. the Landing and the Birches were in great shape for beginners. I saw lots of smiles on the hill - Our mountain ops teams has truly pulled off a miracle getting the mountain in shape for all skiers and riders.

Snowmakers will be working through the night on Lower Comp, Sluice, and Peavey Crosscut. 

Groomers will work on everything we currently have open, in addition to Narrow Gauge, Sluice, Bridle Chain, and Timberline. Bullwinkles will be opening tomorrow, so they'll make sure that the whole area is accessible. 

Tomorrow's lifts are Skidway, Sawduster, DRC-B, and Superquad. Snubber is anticipated to open on Saturday. The groomers will be putting some time into building up the snubber mid-station in the meantime to allow a weekend opening.

Our park crew managed to get both terrain parks back open (Upper Tote Park and Upper Double Bitter)- Shout out to them for making it happen despite the odds. A slight hike from Superquad and you could get to both.

Please note there are natural hazards around the hill given the recent flooding. Remain cautious, both on and off the hill. Watch for water bars, bare spots, and inconsistent surfaces around the mountain.

Our food and beverage team, along with other off-mountain departments have been doing an incredible job with limited staffing - It's been an all-hands-on-deck sort of week. Thank you to the Sugarloafers and the Franklin County community for banding together through this challenging time. It's times like this when I realize how strong this community and the entire State of Maine are.

The mountain operations are pretty much back to status-quo, including retail, rentals, lessons, and repair. Food options are also back open. including 45 North, Narrow Gauge Food Court, Widowmaker, and Bullwinkles tomorrow.

Remember our community as well as neighboring towns still may have active recovery efforts taking place.  Be sure to double-check your "normal" route to Sugarloaf before traveling if you have questions as to whether or not it may be open.  Two good resources are:

Franklin County EMA:  www.franklincountyema.org
DOT map:  New England 511 - MaineDOT

I am so happy to see everyone out there having fun and enjoying what Sugarloaf has to offer.

Ski ya later,

Erica J.

-----------------------------------
Please note that Uphill Travel is closed.  Patrol will reestablish a plan for uphill access when it makes sense. We appreciate your understanding. Reminder: When Uphill Access is permitted, a ticket or season pass is required.  Updates surrounding uphill access will be posted at the bottom of the SuperQuad lift - always check in before you begin your approach.
