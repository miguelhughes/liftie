Today's Snow Reporter: Aanika

Thursday, Feb. 29, 2024:

11 a.m. update: 

Things are warming up, but the backside has still been running really nicely. Snow in the trees has been a tad dense but after your first turn, when you’ve gathered enough speed and power, it becomes a smooth glide. I’m currently loving Flower Point, where conditions feel a little more protected and consistent.

Hope you have a great day out there!

9 a.m. update:

If you’re hoping to come up today (as you should), try to come up early if you can! The snow is so smooth and easy and holding up much better than I had anticipated, though temps are expected to warm up throughout the day.

The wind is doing its thing and we are seeing large whalebacks (snowdrifts) in exposed areas. There is also low visibility on the peak, so please take it slow! Those snowdrifts come out of nowhere!

6:30 a.m. report:

Who else was able to make it up for some snowy runs yesterday? It was so much fun bouncing between soft turns and untouched pockets of powder. And the snow keeps coming: We've received another 5 inches at the summit and 3 inches in the base area over the past 24 hours!

We're also seeing some warmer temperatures and some strong winds, so I'd expect heavier, creamier snow at lower elevations and colder, lighter snow as you get closer to the summit! Like yesterday, there will be some degree of wind transporting so keep an eye out for varying depths.

Today is the Special Olympics Montana Regional Winter Games. Come up to cheer on the athletes!

Also consider signing up to volunteer for our Biggie Banks Boardercross & Skiercross or our final Tommy Moe Kids Race League, both happening this weekend. You get a free lift ticket for your services which can be used within a year of your volunteer date! You can learn more about this weekend's events at our events page. 

In case you missed it, we've rolled out a new way to save on daily lift access! Our $299 Spring Flex 4-Pass is good for four full days on the slopes — any four days you choose to visit! That adds up to a 23% savings compared to our regular adult lift tickets. But hurry! Passes are available online only through Friday, March 1. Click here to learn more and buy your pass today. It's a great way to take advantage of all this new snow!

If you're in need of fresh gear, don't miss our Midwinter Retail Sale! We're taking 20% off everything you need to hit the slopes, including skis, boots, bindings, pants, jackets, bibs, gloves, base layers, goggles and helmets! Check out Snow Ghost Outfitters, Base Lodge Traders or the Shop at the Summit House to see what's in store. New skis and boots are 20% off at Village Rentals as well. Don't miss out!

And finally, consider taking the free S.N.O.W. Bus from town or carpooling if you're headed up with friends. It's a great way to save money and socialize.

Thank you for reading and let's get 'er going!

The snow report is a subjective assessment of terrain and weather conditions. Snowfall and snow depths are measured at the summit and base areas around 6 a.m. each day. Actual depths may vary across the mountain and change throughout the day. Always check with ski patrol if you need more information about skiing conditions.

If you go out of bounds ...

When you exit the marked boundaries of the resort, you are entering backcountry terrain where natural and human-caused avalanches can occur. The resort does not conduct avalanche mitigation on those slopes. Always check the Flathead Avalanche Center's daily avalanche forecast and carry a beacon, shovel and probe.
